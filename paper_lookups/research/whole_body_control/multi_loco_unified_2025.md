## Abstract Summary
Multi-Loco proposes a unified diffusion-based policy that controls bipedal, quadrupedal, and humanoid robots with a single neural network. The central insight is that locomotion across different morphologies shares fundamental principles—balance, gait generation, terrain adaptation—that can be captured by a shared representation. Rather than training separate policies per morphology, Multi-Loco uses zero-padding, binary masks, and per-morphology normalization to align heterogeneous observation and action spaces into a common input/output format compatible with a single diffusion policy network.

The diffusion policy formulation generates action sequences through iterative denoising, starting from Gaussian noise and progressively refining into executable joint commands. This generative approach naturally handles multi-modality in the action distribution—different morphologies may require fundamentally different action patterns for the same locomotion objective, and the diffusion process can represent this diversity without mode collapse. The method draws on Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models (DDPM) adapted for continuous action generation.

Multi-Loco is trained on a combined dataset of trajectories from bipedal (Cassie-like), quadrupedal (A1, Go1), and humanoid (H1) robots in simulation. The unified policy matches or approaches the performance of morphology-specific specialist policies on each platform, while enabling positive transfer between morphologies. Real-world transfer is demonstrated on quadruped hardware, with the unified policy achieving comparable sim-to-real performance to specialist policies.

## Core Contributions
- Demonstrates the first unified locomotion policy controlling bipeds, quadrupeds, and humanoids with a single network
- Introduces a morphology-agnostic input/output alignment strategy using zero-padding, binary masks, and per-morphology normalization
- Applies diffusion policy (DDPM-based) to locomotion, showing that generative modeling naturally handles multi-modal action distributions across morphologies
- Shows positive transfer between morphologies—shared training improves data efficiency and robustness compared to isolated training
- Validates real-world transfer of the unified policy on quadruped hardware
- Achieves within 5-10% of specialist policy performance across all morphologies while using a single set of network weights
- Provides extensive ablation studies on the contribution of each alignment component (padding, masks, normalization)

## Methodology Deep-Dive
The input alignment strategy is the foundation of Multi-Loco. Each morphology has different observation and action dimensionalities: quadrupeds have 12 joints, Cassie has 10 actuated joints, and humanoids have 19+ joints. To feed these into a single network, observations are padded to the maximum dimensionality (humanoid) with zeros, and binary masks indicate which dimensions are active. Per-morphology normalization ensures that the statistical properties of each morphology's observations are compatible: each morphology's observations are independently standardized (zero mean, unit variance) based on its own dataset statistics before padding.

The diffusion policy architecture follows DDPM adapted for continuous control. The forward process adds Gaussian noise to ground-truth action sequences over T diffusion steps: a_t = √(ᾱ_t) a_0 + √(1-ᾱ_t) ε, where ε ~ N(0,I) and ᾱ_t is the cumulative noise schedule. The reverse process is a learned denoiser: a_{t-1} = (1/√α_t)(a_t - (β_t/√(1-ᾱ_t)) ε_θ(a_t, o, t)) + σ_t z, where ε_θ is the noise prediction network conditioned on observation o and diffusion timestep t.

The denoiser ε_θ is a U-Net-like architecture with 1D temporal convolutions operating on action sequences. The observation o is injected via FiLM (Feature-wise Linear Modulation) conditioning layers. The morphology type is embedded as a learnable vector and concatenated with the observation, providing explicit morphology context. The action sequence length is H=16 timesteps, with the first 8 timesteps executed and the remaining 8 providing a planning horizon for temporal consistency.

Training minimizes the noise prediction MSE: L = E_{a_0, ε, t} ||ε - ε_θ(a_t, o, t)||². The dataset contains trajectories from all morphologies, sampled uniformly during training. No reward signals are used—the method is purely imitation-based, learning from expert demonstrations generated by morphology-specific specialist RL policies.

Inference requires T denoising steps per action generation. To meet real-time requirements, DDIM (Denoising Diffusion Implicit Models) acceleration is used, reducing T from 100 to 10 steps with minimal quality loss. Additionally, temporal action caching reuses partially-denoised actions from the previous step as warm starts, further reducing the denoising steps needed to 4-6 per control cycle.

## Key Results & Numbers
- Quadruped locomotion: unified policy achieves 94% of specialist performance on flat terrain, 89% on rough terrain
- Bipedal locomotion (Cassie-like): unified policy achieves 91% of specialist performance on flat terrain
- Humanoid locomotion: unified policy achieves 88% of specialist performance on flat terrain
- Positive transfer: training with 3 morphologies improves bipedal rough-terrain performance by 7% compared to bipedal-only training
- Real-world quadruped: 85% success rate for the unified policy vs. 89% for the specialist policy on Go1 hardware
- Diffusion inference: 10 DDIM steps at 50 Hz control → 200 denoising operations/second, achievable on NVIDIA Jetson
- Training: 500K gradient steps on combined dataset of 1M trajectories (total), ~8 hours on 4× A100 GPUs
- Ablation: removing binary masks degrades bipedal performance by 15%; removing per-morphology normalization degrades all morphologies by 8-12%

## Relevance to Project A (Mini Cheetah Quadruped RL)
**Rating: High**
Multi-Loco demonstrates that a universal locomotion policy can encompass Mini Cheetah's morphology alongside other robots. This has two implications: (1) Mini Cheetah could benefit from positive transfer by training alongside other morphologies, leveraging locomotion principles learned from bipeds and humanoids; (2) the diffusion policy paradigm offers an alternative to PPO-based training, potentially providing better action diversity and smoothness through the generative modeling approach.

The input alignment strategy (zero-padding, binary masks, normalization) provides a practical recipe for handling Mini Cheetah's observation space within a multi-morphology framework. The diffusion policy's ability to generate smooth, multi-modal action trajectories is particularly relevant for locomotion, where action smoothness directly affects hardware wear and energy consumption. The demonstrated real-world transfer on comparable quadruped hardware validates deployability.

## Relevance to Project B (Cassie Bipedal Hierarchical RL)
**Rating: High**
Multi-Loco is directly relevant to Cassie's project in several ways. First, the Cassie-like bipedal platform is explicitly included in the evaluation, providing direct performance benchmarks. Second, the diffusion policy paradigm offers a compelling alternative to PPO for generating locomotion actions at the Controller level of Cassie's hierarchy—the generative model naturally handles multi-modality (different gaits, recovery strategies) without the mode collapse issues of unimodal Gaussian policies.

The shared representation learning across morphologies could enhance Cassie's training by leveraging quadruped locomotion data for pre-training. The input alignment strategy using binary masks could be adapted for Cassie's hierarchical architecture, where different levels produce actions of different dimensionalities. The diffusion policy's planning horizon (H=16 steps with partial execution) provides implicit short-horizon planning that could complement or replace the Planner level's explicit planning.

## What to Borrow / Implement
- Implement diffusion policy (DDPM/DDIM) as an alternative to PPO-based policy for locomotion action generation at the Controller level
- Adopt the zero-padding + binary mask + per-morphology normalization strategy for handling variable-dimensional inputs across Cassie's hierarchical levels
- Use DDIM acceleration and temporal action caching for real-time diffusion policy inference on onboard compute
- Explore multi-morphology co-training: train Mini Cheetah and Cassie policies jointly to leverage positive transfer
- Incorporate FiLM conditioning for injecting task/morphology/terrain context into the policy network

## Limitations & Open Questions
- Diffusion inference is inherently slower than single-forward-pass MLP policies; real-time performance requires DDIM acceleration and caching, which add implementation complexity
- The imitation-based training requires high-quality expert demonstrations, limiting applicability to tasks where expert policies are unavailable
- The unified policy underperforms specialists by 5-12%, raising the question of whether the shared representation imposes a capacity bottleneck
- Scalability to more diverse morphologies (e.g., hexapods, snake robots) and tasks beyond locomotion is untested
