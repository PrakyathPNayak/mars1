# Reduced-Order Model Guided Contact-Implicit MPC for Humanoid Walking

**Authors:** ROM-CIMPC Research Team
**Year:** 2024 | **Venue:** arXiv
**Links:** [Project Page](https://rom-cimpc.github.io/)

---

## Abstract Summary
This paper introduces a two-tier planning framework that combines the computational efficiency of reduced-order models (ROM) with the expressiveness of contact-implicit model predictive control (CI-MPC) for humanoid walking. The core idea is to use a Hybrid Linear Inverted Pendulum (H-LIP) model to rapidly generate nominal center-of-mass trajectories and footstep plans, which then serve as warm-start references for a full-body CI-MPC that refines trajectories while respecting the complete multi-contact dynamics.

The Hybrid LIP provides a closed-form stepping controller that maps the current CoM state to desired footstep locations, accounting for the discrete switching dynamics of bipedal walking. This ROM-level plan is computationally cheap (microseconds) and provides dynamically consistent nominal gaits. The CI-MPC layer then takes this reference and optimizes over the full rigid-body dynamics, automatically adjusting contact forces, timings, and whole-body configurations to track the ROM plan while satisfying all physical constraints.

The combined framework achieves robust multi-contact humanoid walking with real-time disturbance recovery. When perturbed, the H-LIP rapidly re-plans footstep locations, and the CI-MPC adapts the whole-body motion accordingly. This hierarchical decomposition makes the problem tractable while maintaining the richness of contact-implicit optimization.

## Core Contributions
- Hierarchical planning architecture combining Hybrid LIP stepping controller with full-body CI-MPC
- Closed-form footstep adaptation using the H-LIP deadbeat controller for rapid re-planning after disturbances
- Warm-starting CI-MPC with ROM-generated trajectories, reducing convergence time by 3–5×
- Multi-contact walking framework supporting heel-strike, flat-foot, and toe-off phases
- Disturbance recovery demonstrations with pushes up to 30% body weight on humanoid models
- Analysis of when ROM guidance improves CI-MPC convergence vs. when it constrains the solution

## Methodology Deep-Dive
The Hybrid Linear Inverted Pendulum (H-LIP) model captures the essential sagittal-plane dynamics of bipedal walking as a piecewise-linear system. During single support, the CoM dynamics follow: ẍ = (g/z₀)(x − p), where x is the CoM position, p is the stance foot position, g is gravity, and z₀ is the constant CoM height. The hybrid transition at foot strike maps pre-impact to post-impact states via a reset map that updates the stance foot. The H-LIP deadbeat controller computes the next footstep location as: p* = x⁺ + (1/σ)ẋ⁺, where σ = √(g/z₀) and (x⁺, ẋ⁺) are the post-impact states. This yields a one-step deadbeat convergence to the desired periodic orbit.

The CI-MPC layer formulates the whole-body trajectory optimization as: min Σ ‖xₜ − xₜʳᵉᶠ‖²_Q + ‖uₜ‖²_R, subject to full rigid-body dynamics with complementarity contact constraints. The reference trajectory xₜʳᵉᶠ is generated by the H-LIP, lifted to the full state space by inverse kinematics. The contact-implicit formulation allows the CI-MPC to deviate from the ROM-predicted contact timing if the full-body dynamics require it—for instance, extending a swing phase to maintain balance.

The warm-starting procedure maps the ROM solution to the CI-MPC decision variables. CoM trajectories directly initialize the floating-base states, footstep plans initialize contact positions, and the H-LIP's analytical contact forces (ground reaction forces derived from the pendulum model) initialize the contact force variables. Joint configurations are obtained via a fast inverse kinematics solve. This structured warm-start places the CI-MPC optimizer near a good local minimum, dramatically reducing iteration counts.

The disturbance recovery pipeline operates as follows: (1) external push detected via IMU/force sensing, (2) H-LIP re-computes footstep plan in <1 ms, (3) CI-MPC re-optimizes whole-body trajectory with new reference, (4) joint-level PD controller tracks CI-MPC output. The H-LIP's rapid re-planning provides immediate course correction while the CI-MPC ensures physical feasibility.

The framework also handles multi-contact phases by extending the H-LIP model to account for double-support periods. During double support, both feet are in contact, and the CoM dynamics are modulated by the distribution of ground reaction forces between the two feet. The CI-MPC optimizes this force distribution to minimize joint torques while tracking the ROM reference.

## Key Results & Numbers
- H-LIP footstep planning in <0.5 ms, enabling rapid disturbance response
- CI-MPC solve time reduced from 15 ms (cold start) to 3–5 ms (ROM warm-started) per iteration
- Stable walking at speeds from 0.2 to 1.0 m/s on flat terrain in simulation
- Recovery from lateral pushes up to 30% body weight (50 N on a 25 kg humanoid model)
- Tracking error <2 cm CoM deviation from reference during steady-state walking
- Multi-contact gait with heel-strike, flat-foot, and toe-off phases demonstrated

## Relevance to Project A (Mini Cheetah Quadruped RL)
**Rating: Medium**
While primarily designed for bipedal humanoids, the reduced-order model concept transfers to quadruped planning. A simplified centroidal dynamics model (analogous to the H-LIP but for four-legged gaits) could provide trajectory references for the Mini Cheetah's PPO policy. The hierarchical warm-starting strategy is applicable when combining model-based planning with RL—the ROM can generate reference trajectories that guide the RL exploration. The multi-contact phase handling is relevant to quadruped gaits with varying support patterns (trot, bound, gallop). However, the H-LIP specifically is less applicable to quadrupeds than to bipeds.

## Relevance to Project B (Cassie Bipedal Hierarchical RL)
**Rating: High**
This paper is critically relevant to Cassie's hierarchical architecture. The H-LIP model directly applies to Cassie's sagittal-plane walking dynamics and can serve as the foundation for the Planner level's footstep generation. The Capture Point computation in Cassie's safety layer is essentially the H-LIP deadbeat controller—the instantaneous capture point is p* = x + ẋ/σ, which matches the H-LIP stepping formula. The ROM-guided CI-MPC paradigm maps cleanly onto the Planner→Controller hierarchy: H-LIP operates at the Planner level for rapid footstep adaptation, while CI-MPC operates at the Controller level for whole-body torque generation. The disturbance recovery pipeline provides a template for Cassie's reactive balance control. The warm-starting strategy can be used to accelerate the CBF-QP safety filter by providing good initial contact force estimates.

## What to Borrow / Implement
- Implement the H-LIP deadbeat stepping controller as Cassie's primary footstep planner at the Planner level
- Use the ROM warm-starting strategy to accelerate the CI-MPC solver in the Controller level
- Adopt the multi-contact phase framework for modeling Cassie's heel-strike and toe-off transitions
- Leverage the H-LIP's capture point formula for the CBF-QP safety filter's balance constraint
- Apply the hierarchical decomposition principle to separate fast ROM re-planning from slower whole-body optimization

## Limitations & Open Questions
- H-LIP assumes constant CoM height, which is violated during Cassie's knee-bend phases and rough terrain
- The framework is demonstrated only in simulation; hardware validation on actual bipedal robots is not presented
- Limited to relatively flat terrain; extension to stairs, slopes, and unstructured environments is not addressed
- Force distribution during double support assumes quasi-static loading; dynamic transitions may require more sophisticated models
