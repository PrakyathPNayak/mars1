# LocoMuJoCo: A Comprehensive Imitation Learning Benchmark for Locomotion

**Authors:** Al-Hafez et al.
**Year:** 2023 | **Venue:** arXiv
**Links:** [arXiv:2311.02496](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.02496)

---

## Abstract Summary
LocoMuJoCo establishes a standardized benchmark suite for locomotion learning in MuJoCo, addressing the lack of reproducible evaluation protocols in the locomotion RL community. The benchmark encompasses diverse robot morphologies—bipeds, quadrupeds, and musculoskeletal models—with standardized task definitions, observation spaces, action spaces, reward structures, and evaluation metrics. It supports both reinforcement learning and imitation learning paradigms, providing expert demonstration datasets for each task.

The benchmark includes realistic sensor noise models, configurable domain randomization, and systematic difficulty levels, enabling fair comparison of algorithms across the robustness spectrum. Tasks range from flat-terrain walking to complex multi-terrain traversal, with separate evaluation protocols for sample efficiency, asymptotic performance, robustness, and generalization. The authors provide baseline results for major RL and IL algorithms (PPO, SAC, GAIL, DAgger, BC) across all tasks.

LocoMuJoCo fills a critical gap in the locomotion learning literature, where algorithm comparisons are often confounded by differing environments, reward functions, observation spaces, and evaluation criteria. By standardizing these factors, the benchmark enables meaningful comparison of algorithmic contributions in isolation.

## Core Contributions
- Comprehensive MuJoCo-based benchmark spanning biped, quadruped, and musculoskeletal locomotion models
- Standardized observation/action spaces, reward functions, and evaluation metrics for reproducible comparison
- Built-in domain randomization framework with configurable noise levels for robustness evaluation
- Expert demonstration datasets for imitation learning research across all included morphologies
- Baseline results for PPO, SAC, TD3, GAIL, DAgger, and Behavioral Cloning on all benchmark tasks
- Modular architecture allowing easy addition of new robot models and task specifications
- Difficulty progression from flat terrain to stairs, slopes, and uneven ground

## Methodology Deep-Dive
The benchmark is built atop MuJoCo (version 2.3+) and uses the Gymnasium API for consistent interface design. Each task is defined by a tuple (M, O, A, R, T) where M is the robot model (MJCF/URDF), O is the observation space, A is the action space, R is the reward function, and T defines termination conditions. The observation spaces are carefully designed to include proprioceptive information (joint angles, velocities, body orientation via quaternion, angular velocity, contact booleans) and optionally exteroceptive information (heightmap scans, lidar-like range sensors).

Domain randomization is implemented as a configurable pipeline with independent noise sources for: (1) observation noise (Gaussian, σ parameterized per sensor type), (2) dynamics randomization (mass ±10–30%, friction ±20–40%, motor strength ±10–20%), (3) initial state randomization (joint positions ±5–15° from nominal), and (4) terrain randomization (height field generation with configurable roughness). Each noise source can be independently enabled/disabled and its magnitude tuned, enabling systematic study of robustness requirements.

The evaluation protocol defines four metrics computed over N=100 evaluation episodes: (1) **Success Rate**—percentage of episodes where the robot achieves the target velocity for ≥80% of the episode duration; (2) **Normalized Return**—cumulative reward normalized by the maximum achievable return; (3) **Cost of Transport (CoT)**—energy efficiency measured as total work divided by (weight × distance); (4) **Stability Score**—average deviation of the body orientation from upright, penalizing excessive roll/pitch/yaw.

For imitation learning tasks, the benchmark provides expert datasets generated by carefully tuned RL policies (PPO with reward shaping, trained to convergence). Each dataset contains 1000–10000 trajectories with full state-action pairs, enabling comparison of IL algorithms under controlled data budgets. The expert policies achieve >95% success rate, providing a meaningful performance ceiling.

The quadruped models include Unitree A1, ANYmal, and generic quadruped morphologies. Bipedal models include Cassie, Atlas, and simplified walkers. Each model is validated against published dynamic properties (mass, inertia, joint limits, actuator characteristics). The musculoskeletal models provide a bridge to biomechanics research, modeling human-like locomotion with muscle-tendon actuation.

## Key Results & Numbers
- PPO achieves 85–92% success rate on flat-terrain quadruped tasks after 10M environment steps
- SAC matches PPO performance with 30–50% fewer samples on most quadruped tasks
- Domain randomization reduces success rate by 10–25% depending on noise magnitude, revealing algorithm robustness
- Imitation learning (GAIL) reaches 75–85% of RL expert performance with only 100 demonstration trajectories
- Bipedal tasks are significantly harder: PPO achieves 65–80% success rate vs. 85–92% for quadrupeds
- Cost of transport varies 2–3× across algorithms, highlighting efficiency differences invisible in success rate alone
- Benchmark tasks reproduce published results within 5% for PPO and SAC on standard Gymnasium environments

## Relevance to Project A (Mini Cheetah Quadruped RL)
**Rating: High**
LocoMuJoCo provides a directly applicable evaluation framework for the Mini Cheetah project. The quadruped benchmark tasks can serve as standardized testbeds for the Mini Cheetah's PPO-trained locomotion policy, enabling rigorous comparison against published baselines. The domain randomization framework mirrors the Mini Cheetah's sim-to-real strategy and provides a systematic way to characterize robustness.

The benchmark's multi-metric evaluation (success rate + CoT + stability score) is more informative than tracking reward alone, and should be adopted for Mini Cheetah experiments. The expert demonstration datasets could also enable exploration of imitation learning approaches as alternatives or complements to PPO for gait acquisition.

## Relevance to Project B (Cassie Bipedal Hierarchical RL)
**Rating: High**
LocoMuJoCo includes Cassie as one of its bipedal models, making it directly relevant for standardized evaluation of Cassie's hierarchical RL architecture. The benchmark provides a controlled setting to compare Cassie's 4-level hierarchy against flat PPO/SAC baselines, quantifying the benefit of hierarchical decomposition. The bipedal tasks' higher difficulty (65–80% success for PPO vs. 85–92% for quadrupeds) validates the need for more sophisticated approaches like Cassie's architecture.

The domain randomization framework can systematically evaluate the robustness of Cassie's CBF-QP safety layer under varying dynamics. The imitation learning support enables testing of DIAYN/DADS-discovered primitives against benchmark-provided expert demonstrations.

## What to Borrow / Implement
- Adopt LocoMuJoCo's evaluation protocol (success rate, CoT, stability score) as standard metrics for both projects
- Use the domain randomization pipeline for systematic robustness characterization of trained policies
- Leverage Cassie-specific benchmark tasks for head-to-head comparison of hierarchical vs. flat architectures
- Integrate benchmark's observation space definitions to ensure compatibility with published baselines
- Use expert demonstration datasets to bootstrap imitation learning experiments

## Limitations & Open Questions
- Benchmark focuses on steady-state locomotion; transient behaviors (recovery, transitions) are underrepresented
- Terrain diversity limited to procedurally generated heightfields; real-world terrain features (deformable, slippery) not modeled
- Expert demonstrations generated by RL policies may not capture the full diversity of optimal behaviors
- Computational overhead of running full benchmark suite (100 eval episodes × N tasks) may limit rapid iteration
